Website Evaluations

Apple

Apple's website is a prime example of effective web design. It showcases a wide range of products and services with stunning visuals and a user-friendly interface.

  • Contrast: The website excels in contrast with a clean and easily readable design.
  • Repetition: Apple's website maintains a consistent and recognizable design across all pages, reinforcing its brand identity.
  • Alignment: Elements on the site are meticulously aligned, contributing to a visually pleasing and organized layout.
  • Proximity: Content and links are strategically grouped, enhancing user experience and navigation.

The Apple website demonstrates a high level of adherence to the CRAP principles, resulting in a visually appealing and user-friendly online experience.

Craigslist

Craigslist is a notorious example of a poorly designed website. Its outdated interface and lack of user-friendly features make it a frustrating experience for visitors.

  • Contrast: The website lacks sufficient contrast, making text hard to read and causing eye strain.
  • Repetition: Craigslist's design is inconsistent and outdated, with various styles and layouts across different sections.
  • Alignment: Elements on the site are poorly aligned, leading to a cluttered and disorganized appearance.
  • Proximity: Related content is scattered randomly throughout the site, making it challenging for users to find what they need.

Craigslist's website neglects fundamental design principles, resulting in a frustrating and unappealing user experience.
